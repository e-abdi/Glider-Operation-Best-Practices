# Best Practices for Operating Underwater Gliders in Atlantic Canada

[:material-file-pdf-box: Download the paper (PDF)](papers/atlantic-canada-best-practices.pdf)

    Marine Science* 10:1108326. doi:

This review summarizes nearly two decades of glider observing experience
across Atlantic Canada — Memorial University, the Coastal Environmental
Observation Technology and Research (CEOTR) group, and Fisheries and Oceans
Canada (DFO), operating since 2005, 2010, and 2017 respectively. It has
three goals:

1. Give new and emerging glider users guidance and considerations for
   developing a glider program.
2. Review the literature on sensor development for gliders and their
   scientific use.
3. Highlight mission scenarios with enough practical detail to support
   actually operating gliders in the field.

## What It Covers

- Glider operators active in Atlantic Canada and their main projects and
  study areas.
- Regional oceanography: the Newfoundland Shelf, the Gulf of St. Lawrence,
  and related deployment areas.
- **Lessons learned operating gliders in the region** — understanding
  mission costs, glider mission planning, power management, and glider
  emergencies.
- Glider sensor payloads and data streams, including standard corrections
  used across Canadian glider groups.

Since it is written from direct regional operating experience, it is a
useful complement to the platform-specific procedures elsewhere on this
site — particularly for anyone planning missions in the Northwest Atlantic
or facing similar conditions (strong currents, fisheries and shipping
traffic, seasonal ice).

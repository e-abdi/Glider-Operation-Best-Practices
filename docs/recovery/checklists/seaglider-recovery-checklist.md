## 1. Before the boat leaves

- [ ] Pilot: choose the recovery location and fly the glider toward it in advance
- [ ] Field team: notify pilot on departure with ETA on site
- [ ] Pilot: switch to **shallow dives** approaching the rendezvous
- [ ] Pilot: time the `$QUIT` so the glider surfaces just before the boat arrives &emsp;<small>*minimize drift time on the surface*</small>
- [ ] Pilot: confirm glider is in recovery mode and staying on the surface
- [ ] Pilot: relay latest GPS fixes to the field team continuously

## 2. On site

- [ ] Field team: inform pilot on arrival; search the surface
- [ ] Position the boat alongside with the current pushing the boat **away from** the glider &emsp;<small>*never drift onto it*</small>

**Small boat (cradle):**

- [ ] Take control of the glider by the antenna mast
- [ ] Dunk the cradle vertically alongside; float the glider onto it, CT sail facing away from the boat, rudder hooked over the outside of the handle
- [ ] Lift until water drains from the nose; hold until fully drained
- [ ] Bring cradle aboard and secure

**Large vessel (rudder lift):**

- [ ] Rig a large loop of line taped to the end of a long pole
- [ ] Work the loop over the antenna and under the rudder &emsp;<small>*the rudder notches are the **only** lift point on a Seaglider*</small>
- [ ] Lift via winch; pause clear of the water to drain before landing on deck

## 3. Once aboard

- [ ] Inform the pilot the glider is recovered
- [ ] Inspect for visible damage; photograph the as-recovered condition
- [ ] Refit sensor protective covers
- [ ] Power the Seaglider off
- [ ] Pilot: tidy the basestation — mission data into its mission directory; complete the dive log

## 4. Back at port

- [ ] Rinse the glider and all sensors with fresh water
- [ ] Put the glider into **travel mode** &emsp;<small>`hw/misc/travel`</small>
- [ ] Remove rudder and wings; pack for transport

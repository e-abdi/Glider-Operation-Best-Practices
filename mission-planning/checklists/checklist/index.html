    Mission Planning Checklist¶

    Print this page

    Mission title / ID:  _____________________________________________

    PI / Science Lead:  ________________________    Glider S/N:  _______________

    Planned deployment date:  _______________   Planned duration:  _________

    Date of this review:  _______________     Reviewer:  ___________________

    1. Mission Objectives¶

    • Primary science objective defined and recorded in mission plan
    • Required track and/or area of operation specified
    • Required sensors identified and confirmed available
    • Science sampling configuration defined (depth range, yo rate, sampling interval)
    • Mission duration and endurance requirements calculated
    • Secondary / opportunistic objectives identified and prioritised
    • Mission plan signed off by both Science Lead and Glider Logistics Lead

    2. Area of Operations¶

    • Operational boundary polygon defined and recorded

    Tides

    • Tidal range and current speeds assessed for the deployment area
    • Tidal phasing considered in track design and surfacing timing
    • Tidal effects on ballasting target reviewed

    Shipping lanes

    • Shipping lane positions identified (marinetraffic.com or equivalent)
    • Track designed to minimise time in high-traffic corridors
    • AIS monitoring plan confirmed for duration of mission

    Recreational traffic

    • Recreational boating, sailing, and water-sports activity in area assessed
    • Proximity to marinas, race courses, and popular anchorages checked
    • Seasonal recreational patterns considered for deployment timing

    Fishing grounds

    • Active fishing grounds, trawl corridors, and longline areas identified
    • Local fisheries authority or fishing association contacted where relevant
    • Piloting strategy for minimising bottom time in trawled areas confirmed

    Bathymetry & sea floor characteristics

    • Accurate bathymetry chart sourced and reviewed for the full track
    • Minimum water depths along track confirmed adequate for planned dive depth
    • Seamounts, pinnacles, and steep slope areas identified
    • Altimeter target altitude set with appropriate margin for local seabed roughness
    • Bottom sediment type noted where relevant (soft vs. hard — affects bottom-strike risk)
    • Abort waypoints and emergency recovery areas identified along the track

    Other hazards

    • Active military exercise areas checked (NOTMARs reviewed)
    • Subsea cables, pipelines, and aquaculture installations reviewed
    • Marine Protected Area boundaries checked; operating conditions confirmed

    3. Risk Assessment¶

    • Risk assessment document written or updated for this mission
    • Environmental risks identified and mitigation measures defined
    • Technical risks identified (glider age, sensor reliability, battery state)
    • Operational risks identified (vessel availability, personnel coverage, comms gaps)
    • Insurance availability assessed and arranged if required
    • List of potential emergency recovery vessels and contact points compiled
    • Contingency plan documented (back-up glider, emergency recovery procedure)
    • Risk assessment reviewed and signed off by Mission Lead

    4. Glider & Sensor Preparation¶

    • Maintenance and calibration history reviewed; any preemptive service identified
    • Sensor calibration status confirmed current for all payload sensors
    • Ballasting target calculated using local water density data (CTD, models, or historical records)
    • Ballasting data source recorded: ___________________________________
    • Previous mission notes reviewed for oddities, setting changes, or unresolved faults
    • Glider labels and contact information checked / updated
    • Pre-deployment lab test slot confirmed

    5. Piloting & Communications Plan¶

    • Piloting rota established for at least the first deployment phase
    • Experienced pilot confirmed for launch and first dives
    • Escalation contact list prepared and distributed to all pilots
    • Missed-call protocol defined (number of missed calls before escalation)
    • Communication method between pilot and field team agreed and tested
    • Satellite communication tested with vehicle (link confirmed)
    • Shore-side monitoring schedule documented
    • Piloting alert system verified

    6. Logistics¶

    • Deployment vessel confirmed and booked
    • Staff travel and accommodation arranged
    • Equipment shipping arranged (domestic / international as applicable)
    • Dangerous goods certification confirmed for lithium battery shipment
    • ATA carnet prepared if applicable
    • Third-party recipients of hazardous goods notified

    7. Regulatory & Notifications¶

    • Diplomatic clearance obtained (international deployments): ref _____________
    • NOTMAR / Notice to Mariners filed: ref _____________________________
    • Coastguard notification submitted: ref _____________________________
    • Research permits / environmental authorisations obtained: ref ____________
    • Harbour master notified (if deploying from port): ref ___________________
    • All confirmation receipts archived in mission folder

    8. Data & Metadata¶

    • Data flow set up with regional Data Assembly Centre (DAC)
    • Minimum OceanOPS metadata fields completed (programme, WMO ID, PI, deployment position, agency, sensor models, glider model, serial number)
    • Metadata submitted to OceanOPS ahead of deployment
    • File naming conventions and repository structure confirmed with DAC
    • Automated data transfer tested end-to-end

    9. Final Review¶

    • Mission plan reviewed by a team member not involved in its preparation
    • All team members briefed on plan, contingencies, and abort criteria
    • Health & Safety briefing completed; SOP signed by all personnel
    • Mission plan archived in designated location
    • Go / No-Go authority confirmed: ___________________________________

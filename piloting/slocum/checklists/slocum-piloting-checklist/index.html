    Slocum Piloting Checklist¶

    Print this page

    A daily pilot log. Work down the list each piloting day, recording the value from the indicated source and flagging anything outside its normal range.

    Pilot:  ___________________________   Glider S/N:  _____________   Date (UTC):  _____________

    First piloting day of a deployment

    Confirm Science and Navigation against the campaign plan, and create a new important log entry with a fresh campaign summary for reference.

    Status & Identity¶

    Source: glider dialog unless noted.

    • Date
    • Last surface reason
    • Current mission
    • Mission time  paste the seconds timestamp; glider aborts for overtime every 45 days**
    • GPS location
    • Next surfacing is in  estimated from glider history

    Navigation¶

    • Performance of navigation last leg  are we headed to the desired waypoint?
    • Navigation to / why  which waypoint are we going to, and potentially why

    Note

    If the waypoint is not part of the campaign plan, update the campaign plan.

    Devices & Altimeter¶

    • Devices  from SFMC — are any devices in error?
    • Altimeter  is it seeing bottom, and at what depth is it turning on?
    • Number of yos and depth  from SFMC

    Note

    If the in-water navigation has changed from the campaign plan, update the plan.

    Science¶

    • Science  is science data coming back when and how expected for the sensors below — or changed?
    • CTD  compare against campaign plan
    • Sensor 1 __________  name the sensor and compare against campaign plan
    • Sensor 2 __________  name the sensor and compare against campaign plan
    • Sensor 3 __________  name the sensor and compare against campaign plan
    • Sensor 4 __________  name the sensor and compare against campaign plan

    Note

    • If science sampling needed to be changed, update the campaign plan.
    • At least once a week, confirm the sampling of all science sensors against the Science Campaign plan.

    Mission Script & Power Modes¶

    • Script  copy from terminal
    • Current correction  on or off, from dialog
    • Lower power mode  off, or the value in use, from glider dialog

    Note

    If the script needs to change or is having difficulty, add a comment to Notes below and include it in the handover / notification to the lead pilot.

    Energy & Consumption¶

    Comment if too high or too low.

    • Consumption
    • m_lithium_battery_relative_charge (%)
    • m_coulomb_amphr_total (amp-hrs)
    • Expected end date  at 80% and 100%

    Health & Vacuum¶

    • m_vacuum  from glider dialog — typically 8–11
    • Leak-detect voltages  from glider dialog — typically 2.4–2.5
    • Battery surface voltage  from glider dialog
    • Battery min voltage  record and compare against history, looking for outlying results
    • Battery max voltage  record and compare against history, looking for outlying results

    Flight & Ballast¶

    • Dive profiles symmetric  True / False — if false, how and why
    • Autoballast converged  True / False — if false, how and why; add climb and dive CC here
    • m_avg_speed  from glider dialog — watch for trend changes
    • Pitch / Roll  from SFMC — watch for trend changes
    • Battpos  from SFMC — watch for trend changes

    Campaign & Notes¶

    • Has the campaign plan changed and been updated?  why and what? Campaigns can change for PI / distress / feature / other

      Note

      If yes — update the Campaign Log and mark important.

    • Notes  free-form — anything we need to keep an eye on?

      Note

      If there is a concern, notify the lead pilot(s), add it to the handover email, or both.

    • Weather  wave and wind speed and direction (e.g. from Windy)

    • Obstacles  ice edge, shallows, shelf edge, etc. — piloting concerns that might lead to a change of mission or campaign

    Saving this log

    • Make sure the location of this log is referenced in the handover email and the campaign plan.
    • If highly customised for a particular campaign, save a new template of the log to the expedition's folder.
    • Typically a pilot saves this template locally and fills it out each day.
